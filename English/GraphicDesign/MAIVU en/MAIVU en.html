High stakes

As my first professional work in Sydney, I felt a strong responsibility to make it exceptional. There was no room for compromise as this wasn't just about meeting the client's expectations; it was about setting the standard for my work in a new country. I couldn't allow myself to deliver something the client might like but I didn't fully stand behind. I put in way more hours than planned, definitely beyond what made sense in terms of time or money. But for me, it had to be perfect, and I wasn't going to let budget get in the way. That dedication is why I look back on my time working on Mai Vu with so much pride. It remains one of my favourite logo and branding designs to date and a defining moment in my work.

A journey through coffee

Mai Vu was an emerging fine jewellery brand looking to set itself apart in a competitive space. The founder had spent two years thoughtfully designing a collection inspired by her own personal journey, one rooted in authenticity, resilience, and the beauty of growth through life's highs and lows. She wanted a logo that could capture all of that: not just a brand mark, but a symbol of strength, journey, and transformation. The brief wasn't overly prescriptive, which made the early stages a bit open-ended. I could tell there was a lot of feeling behind the concept, but they were just words without an order, path or shape. What was supposed to be a quick coffee meeting turned into a long, inspiring conversation that lasted until well past lunch. We talked about her roots, her aspirations, the emotional layers behind her work. That changed everything.

All that glitters is not gold

If Mai was certain about something, it was the color palette. Green and gold. Initially, I was hesitant. Gold can be hard to reproduce digitally and lacks the 3D contrast needed to look truly "gold" on screen. While it works well in print and packaging, I wasn't sure how it would translate to a website or social media. The process was tricky, as I didn't want the gold to feel like a dated PowerPoint gradient. After much trial and error, we finally landed on a palette that felt modern and worked across all platforms, one both Mai and I were thrilled with.

What did Mai think?

"I met Gonzalo by chance a couple of months ago, and we connected right away over our love of art and creativity. I had just been thinking that my brand logo needed a facelift, and Gonzalo came at exactly the right time. Running my own jewellery business means that attention to detail and finding a way to stand out in a saturated market is very important to me. I wanted the logo to represent me as the person behind the brand and reflect the story that goes with it. The final delivery was everything I had hoped for and more. Thank you!"

Mai Vu — Founder, Mai Vu Fine Jewellery
